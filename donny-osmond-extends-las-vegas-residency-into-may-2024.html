    Donny Osmond has announced that he’s extending his Las Vegas residency at Harrah’s Hotel & Casino, with dates lined up into May of next year. The singer, who appeared on Season 1 of The Masked Singer, said he wants to “keep the party going.”

    Donny Osmond Announces Extended Las Vegas Residency

    Osmond shared the news with fans on Instagram, announcing dates in Las Vegas from January to May of next year. Fans can buy tickets for the new dates now. Osmond will also be performing at Harrah’s Las Vegas in September, October, and November of this year.

    “So excited to keep the party going,” Osmond wrote to caption his Instagram announcement. According to the LA Times, the singer kicked off the solo residency in 2021. He had previously performed with his sister Marie Osmond for 11 years at Flamingo Las Vegas.

    During a recent interview with Fox News, Osmond said he’s “having so much fun” performing his Vegas residency, so he decided, “Why stop?”

    This summer, Osmond is performing on a North American tour. He has dates lined up through August 13. Later this year, the singer will be bringing his show to the UK with dates in November and December.

    Osmond Placed Second on The Masked Singer

    Back in 2019, Osmond appeared on the first season of The Masked Singer as Peacock. He wowed the panel with his performances of songs such as “The Greatest Show” from The Greatest Showman, “All of Me” by John Legend, and “Can’t Feel My Face” by The Weeknd.

    Osmond finished the competition in second place, behind Monster (T-Pain). Only Jenny McCarthy correctly guessed his identity. The other panelists thought he might be either Neil Patrick Harris or “Weird Al” Yankovic.

    In Season 8, Osmond returned to the show as a guest panelist. He also filled in for panelist Ken Jeong during an episode of The Masked Singer UK.
