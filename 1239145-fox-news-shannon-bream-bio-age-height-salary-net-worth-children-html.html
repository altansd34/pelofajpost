    Shannon Bream's biography

    Shannon is the only daughter of ‎Marie Norris (mother) and ‎Ed DePuy (father). Marie was a teacher, while DePuy was a Leon County Commissioner. The couple raised their girl in Tallahassee, Florida.

    The TV star's brother is a former Navy pilot, and they hail from a humble background. For instance, Shannon would help her mother renovate their house by using a scraper to remove paint from window panes.

    How old is Shannon Bream?

    Shannon Bream's age is 52 years as of June 2023. She was born in Sanford, Florida, USA, on December 23, 1970, as Shannon Noelle DePuy.

    Educational background

    Noelle attended a local primary school in her hometown, Tallahassee, Florida. She graduated from North Florida Christian High School in Tallahassee in 1988.

    The lady joined Liberty University in Lynchburg, Virginia, and won the school's Miss Virginia beauty pageant title in 1990.

    She won the 1995 Miss Florida USA pageant and was fourth in the Miss USA 1995 pageant while studying at law school. After graduating from Florida State University College of Law in 1996, Noelle moved to Tampa, Florida, to kick off her law career.

    Career history

    Noelle interned with Bill McCollum, Florida Congressman, in the US House of Representatives after getting her law degree, mainly working on sexual harassment cases.

    She would practice law during the day and interned at WFTS (ABC affiliate) in Tampa, Florida, at night whenever she could. She started at the bottom rung, making coffee and handling phone calls, but lost the job.

    What happened to Shannon Bream?

    She said the boss who fired her would harshly criticize her performance, which helped her grow career-wise. The lady later dropped the legal profession for a full-time TV career in the early 2000s. Furthermore, her passion for current events and telling stories enabled her to land opportunities.

    Noelle moved to Charlotte, North Carolina, in 2001 to work as an evening and late-night news reporter for WBTV (CBS affiliate) in Charlotte, NC. She later joined WRC-TV (NBC affiliate) in Washington, DC, in 2004 after working at WBTV for three years.

    Shannon Bream's Fox News career

    Noelle met Brit Hume while at WRC-TV. Brit was the managing editor of the Fox News Channel's Washington bureau then. He encouraged Shannon to apply for a job at Fox.

    The lady submitted audition tapes to Fox News and landed a job at the network's Washington, DC, bureau in November 2007. Fox assigned her to report the Supreme Court hearing of the US military prison detainees in Guantanamo Bay in 2008.

    Shannon hosted the Fox News @Night with Shannon Bream. It is Fox's first late-night weekday show. The show is currently hosted by Trace Gallagher, who was named permanent host of the program in September of 2022.

    Bream departed to be the anchor of Fox News Sunday. In 2022, she covered the landmark Supreme Court decision in June that overturned Roe v. Wade, the retirement announcement of Supreme Court Justice Stephen Breyer, and more.
