    NBC’s upcoming fall schedule was announced on Wednesday, as a premiere date was revealed for The Voice Season 24. The new season will kick off the same week that America’s Got Talent Season 18 comes to a close.

    The Voice Season 24 Premiere Date Revealed

    According to the newly announced schedule for this fall on NBC, The Voice Season 24 will have a two-night premiere on Monday, September 25, from 8 to 10 p.m., and Tuesday, September 26, from 8 to 9 p.m.

    That same Tuesday, September 26, America’s Got Talent will air the performance portion of the Season 18 finale, from 9 to 11 p.m. Then on Wednesday, September 27, AGT will air the results portion of the finale, once again from 9 to 11 pm.

    AGT is currently still airing auditions for the current season, with the live shows expected to begin on August 22. At that point, the show will move to two nights a week, with the results shows airing on Wednesday nights after an overnight public vote.

    This Season’s Coaches Have Already Been Announced

    When The Voice returns in September for Season 24, it will be the first season without coach Blake Shelton, who left the show earlier this year. Instead, new coach Reba McEntire will be joining the panel, alongside John Legend, Gwen Stefani, and Niall Horan.

    The coaching lineup has also been announced for Season 25, which is expected to premiere in the spring. McEntire and Legend will be back as coaches, alongside Chance the Rapper and the show’s first coaching duo, Dan + Shay.

    Filming of the Blind Auditions for both of the upcoming seasons is underway this month. Are you excited for the new season of The Voice to kick off on Monday, September 25? Let us know in the comments below.
