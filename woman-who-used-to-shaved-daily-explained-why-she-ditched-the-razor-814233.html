    A Woman Who Used to Shave Daily Explained Why She Ditched the Razor

    In a world that often emphasizes smooth, hairless skin as the ideal, it’s important to remember that beauty comes in many different forms. Embracing one’s natural self, including body hair, can be a powerful act of self-acceptance and empowerment. One woman who has embarked on this journey is Nellie-Jean Robinson, a 35-year-old financial analyst from Orlando, Florida.

    She suffers from hirsutism.

    Nellie-Jean suffers from hirsutism, a hormonal imbalance that causes excessive hair growth on a woman’s face or body. Her decision to stop shaving and embrace her facial hair is a testament to her strength and determination to challenge societal beauty standards.

    She despised her facial appearance and made great efforts to conceal it.

    She struggled with her facial hair and the societal pressure to conform to beauty standards for years. She spent a lot of time trying to hide her facial hair and felt more comfortable avoiding situations that would expose it. This affected her self-esteem and made it challenging for her to cultivate romantic relationships. Nellie-Jean always had a disclaimer about touching her face and relied on makeup to conceal the scarring from shaving.

    As she put it, “I spent a lot of years hating the way that I looked, so I was constantly trying to hide it and felt more comfortable going out late to like a comedy club versus going to the beach.”

    She carried razors with her everywhere.

    The constant need to shave and touch up her makeup became a hassle, and she even carried razors and makeup in her purse in case she needed to stay overnight somewhere. This struggle made her question the viability of marriage.

    She explained, “I kept razors and makeup in my purse in the event I ended up being anywhere overnight. It was quite the hassle and really made me feel as if marriage was not a viable option.”

    She struggled to cultivate romantic relationships.

    She struggled with her physical appearance and found it challenging to let men get close to her and be vulnerable. The constant pressure to hide her facial hair and scarring made it difficult for her to cultivate romantic relationships. As she expressed, “It also made it hard to cultivate romantic relationships, making it difficult for me to grow close to men physically, intimately, and romantically.”

    Her fear of judgment and rejection created barriers that prevented her from fully opening up and connecting with others on a deeper level. It was a constant battle between her desire for love and companionship and her fear of being unable to meet societal standards.

    She found freedom in rejecting conventional beauty standards.

    In 2021, Nellie-Jean boldly embraced her natural hair growth despite her initial fears. As she went razor-free for a few weeks, she soon realized it wasn’t as bad as she had feared. In fact, it was a liberating experience that allowed her to break free from the constant pressure of shaving and wearing makeup.

    Reflecting on this newfound freedom, she even exclaimed, “This is quite liberating to no longer be a slave to shaving and wearing makeup.” From that point on, she firmly committed herself, confidently stating, “I haven’t put a razor to my face since.”

    Nellie-Jean found healing by sharing her struggles online.

    After her transformative experience of embracing her natural hair growth, Nellie-Jean’s newfound sense of confidence and self-love moved her to share her journey on TikTok. As she opened up about her struggles with hirsutism and PCOS, she received overwhelming praise for her honesty and openness.

    Nellie-Jean shared, “I had a desire to share my story about hirsutism and PCOS because of the freedom and liberty I received from growing my hair out. The shame, embarrassment, and guilt that I experienced up until that point were no longer there.”

    She views her platform as a mission to support women in similar shoes.

    As someone who had battled with insecurity and self-doubt for so long, Nellie-Jean was committed to using her platform to support and uplift other women facing the same struggles. “I know that there are women just like me struggling in silence who deserved to hear my story to know that they were not alone,” she explained.

    Nellie-Jean’s courageous act of vulnerability inspires and empowers others to embrace their authenticity and find liberation from societal expectations.

    As the razor gets tossed aside, a fascinating tapestry of reasons emerges, revealing a paradigm shift that challenges our preconceived notions of beauty and femininity. Stay tuned to uncover the thought-provoking insights behind this gritty decision and its transformative effect on countless women’s lives globally.
